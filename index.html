I did have the feeling and I’ve also been told that in uni they study 10 years old science but I guess this is my process of growing up … rebuilding the world I once knew.

I mentioned nepotism … one more mystery, until not more that than 15 minutes ago I would have said this is a made up Romanian word to describe how at home titles are given in family, we also have the word “nepot” that means nephew, and -ism is a common suffix to target a social behaviour. But yet in english it describes some category of political corruption. Wiki says it comes from the Italian “nepotismo”. If wiki says so, but in Italy it descries an old past and limited phenomenon while in Romanian they are widely used both nepot and nepotism and they also have a grammatically valid link of derivation. Its high presence in today’s language gives the word nepotism a greater chance to influence a germanic language like English and the similarity points in the same direction. But again, who has the power makes the rules, isn’t this one of the implications on nepotism?

Cooking is lethal

According to a study published in Nature Magazine September 2015 air particles smaller than 2.5mc are responsible for an average of 3.3 million premature deaths in 2010.

The interesting fact is that particles of organic provenience bring a lot more casualties than crust material, nitrates or sulphates. But the reason I ended up writing this article is the following phrase: 3.54 million deaths per year caused by indoor air pollution Did I read right? Yes it is INDOOR air pollution, and further on I discover that the outmost evil is cooking. Of course it is about India and China, but it is still difficult to overlook the fact that in China casualties caused by cooking are “approximately an order of magnitude higher than that attributable to Chinese road transport injuries and HIV/AIDS”. I think I made my point ;) be safe, go out!
